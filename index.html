Ali Ncibi

Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the STIC Doctoral School, University of Paris-Saclay, under the supervision of Dr. Luc Bouganim and Professor Philippe Pucheral. I am a member of the PETRUS Team at Inria Saclay and the DAVID Lab at the University of Versailles Saint-Quentin-en-Yvelines. My research focuses on the recognition of indoor Activities of Daily Living (ADL) using sensor-based approaches, with a particular emphasis on privacy-preserving and privacy-friendly binary sensors.

Research Interests

Data-Driven Development of Human Activity Recognition Systems

Specializing in the recognition of indoor Activities of Daily Living (ADL) through sensor-based approaches, with a particular focus on leveraging privacy-preserving binary sensors for accurate and efficient activity recognition.

Keywords: Machine Learning (ML), Internet of Things (IoT), Human Activity Recognition (HAR), Activity Learning (AL)
